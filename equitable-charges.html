What this is

A standalone route for borrowers who are stuck at consent.

An equitable charge can be useful where there is enough equity in the property, but the existing lender will not provide consent for a standard registered second charge in the timeframe required. It is not a loophole or a guaranteed approval. It is a specialist secured funding route that needs careful structuring.

High-street lender says no

Your existing bank or mortgage lender will not grant consent, or the process is taking too long for the commercial deadline.

The equity is there

The property has enough equity to support a short-term secured facility, but a normal bank bridge is not moving quickly enough.

We route the case

If equitable charge is right, we progress it. If a consented second charge, Speed Loan or Bank Bridge is better, we tell you straight.

Important: equitable charge lending is assessed case by case. Existing lender position, property type, borrower profile, purpose of funds, LTV, exit strategy and legal enforceability all matter. Your property may be repossessed if you do not keep up repayments on any debt secured against it.
Where it fits

Not Speed Loan. Not a standard second charge. A separate specialist path.

Speed Loan is for fastest low-LTV liquidity. Bank Bridge is for institutional bridging. Equitable Charges sits between the two when the practical issue is lender consent — especially where the borrower has already been told consent is unavailable or too slow.

  • Borrower has property equity but existing mortgage consent is the blocker.
  • Suitable where speed and structure matter more than a vanilla lender process.
  • Works best with a clean, evidenced exit: sale, refinance, transaction proceeds or business cashflow.
  • We may still route to a regulated/unregulated bridge, consented second charge or Speed Loan if more appropriate.
