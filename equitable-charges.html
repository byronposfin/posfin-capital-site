What is an equitable charge?

In plain English: it is a form of secured interest in property, commonly protected by registering a unilateral notice at HM Land Registry — sometimes called a “soft charge”. It is different from a standard legal charge and can give the borrower a different protection profile because the lender’s remedies are not usually the same direct legal-charge power-of-sale route. That is why lender risk is higher — and why it is a specialist product.

This still creates a secured debt. Default can have serious consequences and every case needs legal review. The value for the borrower is simple: where a normal consented second charge is blocked, an equitable charge may still unlock liquidity quickly.
