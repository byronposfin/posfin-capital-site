What We Fund

Three scenarios where Dev Exit finance solves the problem

Development Exit is specifically structured for schemes at practical completion — where the development loan needs to come off the asset.

01
Completed scheme awaiting sale
Development is finished but units haven't sold yet. The development loan rate is expensive. Dev Exit refinances at a lower rate and buys you time to sell at the right price.
02
Development loan expiring
Your lender's term is running out and they want repayment, but sales aren't complete. Dev Exit gives you a clean exit from the dev facility and breathing room to complete disposals.
03
Refinance off development loan
Some developers refinance off the development facility at practical completion as standard — locking in the lower exit rate and releasing any equity above the development cost.

The Process

Faster than dev finance — scheme is already built

Because the scheme is complete, there's no build monitoring required. That means a simpler process and faster drawdown — typically around 15 days.

01
Enquiry & indicative terms — same day
Tell us the scheme, the GDV, and what needs to come off. We assess the deal and issue indicative exit terms — rate, facility size, and LTV — within hours.
02
Valuation — Week 1
Lender instructs a desktop or RICS valuation on the completed scheme. Because units are built and comparable evidence exists, this is typically faster than during construction.
03
Legal — Week 1–2
Solicitors handle the charge transfer and redemption of the existing development loan. Where Ackroyd Legal are instructed, this process runs efficiently alongside the valuation.
04
Drawdown — Day 12–15
Facility drawn, development loan redeemed. You're now on the lower exit rate with a clear timeline to sell each unit. Partial redemptions typically available as units sell.
